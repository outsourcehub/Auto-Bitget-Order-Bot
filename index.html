Core Trading Concepts

Understanding Spot Trading on BitGet

Spot trading on BitGet involves the direct purchase or sale of cryptocurrencies at the current market price. Key concepts:

  • Trading pairs (e.g., BTC/USDT) represent the assets being exchanged
  • Order books show available buy and sell orders at different price levels
  • Market depth indicates liquidity at various price points
  • Spread is the difference between the highest buy and lowest sell price

Market Orders

Market orders execute immediately at the best available price:

  • Execute immediately at the best available price
  • Guarantee execution but not exact price
  • Used when speed is more important than exact price
  • Command: /buy BTC/USDT 100 (Buy $100 worth of BTC)

Stop-Loss Explained

A stop-loss is an automated order to sell your position when the price drops to a specified level. The stop loss of this bot can be set before a trade happens (fixed):

  • Helps limit potential losses by automatically exiting losing positions
  • Set as a percentage below your purchase price
  • Example: 5% stop-loss on BTC purchased at $30,000 will trigger a sell at $28,500
  • Command: /setstoploss 5 (Set a 5% stop-loss)

Alerts with Auto-Buy and Stop-Loss Configuration

Trailing Stop-Loss Strategy

Trailing stop-loss automatically adjusts your stop-loss level as the price moves in your favor. The trailing stop of this bot can be set before a trade happens (fixed):

  • Initial stop-loss is set at a percentage below purchase price
  • As price increases, the stop-loss level rises accordingly
  • If price falls, stop-loss level remains fixed
  • Helps lock in profits while allowing room for normal market volatility
  • Command: /settrailingstop 10 2 (Set trigger at 10% and callback at 2%)

Alerts with Auto-Buy and Trailing Stop-Loss Configuration

Risk Management Fundamentals

Effective risk management is essential for successful trading:

  • Never risk more than you can afford to lose on a single trade
  • Use auto stop-loss/trailing orders on every position
  • Diversify across multiple cryptocurrencies
  • Consider risk-reward ratio (potential profit vs. potential loss)
  • Maintain a trading journal to track performance and improve strategies

Configuring Trailing Stop-Loss

Understanding Trigger and Callback Percentages

Trigger Percentage: The amount the price must increase before the trailing stop activates
Callback Percentage: How far the price can fall from its peak before triggering a sell

Example:

  • Buy BTC at $20,000
  • Set trailing stop with 10% trigger and 2% callback
  • Trailing stop activates when BTC reaches $22,000 (10% increase)
  • If BTC reaches $25,000 then falls to $24,500 (2% drop), the position is sold

Practical Trailing Stop Examples

Set up trailing stop with custom parameters:

This sets a trailing stop with 15% trigger and 3% callback for all monitored positions.

Whitelist Management

The whitelist ensures you only receive alerts containing specific terms. When your whitelist has entries, you'll only receive alerts that match at least one of those entries.

Add Coin to Whitelist

/wl_add BTC

After adding BTC, you'll only receive BTC-related alerts.

Add Trading Pattern

/wl_add "bull flag"

Filter alerts to only show those mentioning specific patterns. Use quotes for terms with spaces.

View Your Whitelist

/wl_view

Displays all your current whitelist entries.

Remove from Whitelist

/wl_remove BTC

Removes a specific entry from your whitelist.

Clear Entire Whitelist

/wl_clear

Removes all entries, disabling whitelist filtering.

Managing the whitelist to filter which alert notifications you receive

Blacklist Management

The blacklist lets you exclude alerts containing specific terms. Any alert matching a blacklist entry will be filtered out, regardless of other criteria.

Add Coin to Blacklist

/bl_add SHIB

Blocks all SHIB-related alerts from reaching you.

Add Pattern to Blacklist

/bl_add "weak breakout"

Filter out alerts containing specific patterns you want to avoid.

View Your Blacklist

/bl_view

Shows all terms currently in your blacklist.

Remove from Blacklist

/bl_remove SHIB

Removes a specific entry from your blacklist.

Clear Entire Blacklist

/bl_clear

Removes all entries, turning off blacklist filtering completely.

Advanced Trading Strategies

There are many ways to use the bot. Here are 4 popular methods. Follow our dedicated educational channel for more at https://t.me/BigetSpotTrader

1. Manual Buy + Channel Alerts

In this method, the /togglealerts is off. You don't receive any alerts or auto buy in your auto order bot. When you choose to, you manually go through the channel and select what to buy. The trailing stop, stop loss, and auto amount are all set. This method gives you full control over what you buy.

2. Personalized Alerts + Auto Buy + Tight Stop Loss

In this method, you have /togglealerts turned on with no whitelist/blacklist setup, so you receive all alerts to buy all tokens. You set a reasonable trailing stop to give the token a chance during volatility and set a tight stop loss.

3. Personalized Alerts + Auto Buy + Black/Whitelist + Tight Stop Loss

This is similar to the previous method, but here the blacklist/whitelist is active for more confidence in the alerts, though not very strict. The goal remains the same: take as much profit as you can and exit when the price moves in the wrong direction.

4. Strict Black/Whitelist + Personalized Alerts + Auto Buy

This method relies on strict whitelist/blacklist rather than tight stop loss, /togglealerts turned on. It gives the stop loss and trailing stop more space, as the user trusts that the token is pumping and likely volatile, so it provides more room for price movement.
