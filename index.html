Core Trading Concepts

Understanding Spot Trading on BitGet

Spot trading on BitGet involves the direct purchase or sale of cryptocurrencies at the current market price. Key concepts:

  • Trading pairs (e.g., BTC/USDT) represent the assets being exchanged
  • Order books show available buy and sell orders at different price levels
  • Market depth indicates liquidity at various price points
  • Spread is the difference between the highest buy and lowest sell price

Market Orders

Market orders execute immediately at the best available price:

  • Execute immediately at the best available price
  • Guarantee execution but not exact price
  • Used when speed is more important than exact price
  • Command: /buy BTC/USDT 100 (Buy $100 worth of BTC)

Stop-Loss Explained

A stop-loss is an automated order to sell your position when the price drops to a specified level. The stop loss of this bot can be set before a trade happens (fixed):

  • Helps limit potential losses by automatically exiting losing positions
  • Set as a percentage below your purchase price
  • Example: 5% stop-loss on BTC purchased at $30,000 will trigger a sell at $28,500
  • Command: /setstoploss 5 (Set a 5% stop-loss)

Trailing Stop-Loss Strategy

Trailing stop-loss automatically adjusts your stop-loss level as the price moves in your favor. The trailing stop of this bot can be set before a trade happens (fixed):

  • Initial stop-loss is set at a percentage below purchase price
  • As price increases, the stop-loss level rises accordingly
  • If price falls, stop-loss level remains fixed
  • Helps lock in profits while allowing room for normal market volatility
  • Command: /settrailingstop 10 2 (Set trigger at 10% and callback at 2%)

Risk Management Fundamentals

Effective risk management is essential for successful trading:

  • Never risk more than you can afford to lose on a single trade
  • Use auto stop-loss/trailing orders on every position
  • Diversify across multiple cryptocurrencies
  • Consider risk-reward ratio (potential profit vs. potential loss)
  • Maintain a trading journal to track performance and improve strategies

Configuring Trailing Stop-Loss

Understanding Trigger and Callback Percentages

Trigger Percentage: The amount the price must increase before the trailing stop activates
Callback Percentage: How far the price can fall from its peak before triggering a sell

Example:

  • Buy BTC at $20,000
  • Set trailing stop with 10% trigger and 2% callback
  • Trailing stop activates when BTC reaches $22,000 (10% increase)
  • If BTC reaches $25,000 then falls to $24,500 (2% drop), the position is sold

Practical Trailing Stop Examples

Set up trailing stop with custom parameters:

/settrailingstop 15 3

This sets a trailing stop with 15% trigger and 3% callback for all monitored positions.

Understanding Alert Types and Signals

The bot recognizes several types of breakout patterns:

  • Support/Resistance Breakouts: Price breaking through established barriers
  • Moving Average Crossovers: Short-term MA crossing above long-term MA
  • Volume Spikes: Unusual trading volume with price movement
  • Bullish Patterns: Common chart patterns like bull flags or cup and handle
  • Multi-Timeframe Confirmations: When multiple timeframes show bullish signals

Alert Classification System

Alerts are classified by strength:

  • Strong Alerts: High probability setups with multiple confirming factors
  • Moderate Alerts: Good setups with some confirming factors
  • Weak Alerts: Early signals that need further confirmation

Classification is based on:

  • Number of technical criteria met
  • Volume confirmation
  • Price momentum
  • Historical pattern reliability
  • Multi-timeframe alignment

Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA) Strategy

Dollar-Cost Averaging is a strategy that involves regularly buying fixed amounts of cryptocurrency regardless of market conditions:

  • Reduces impact of short-term volatility
  • Removes emotion from trading decisions
  • Requires long-term investment horizon

Implementation:

  1. Set a regular interval (e.g., weekly or monthly)
  2. Determine a fixed amount to invest each time
  3. Use the /buy command consistently according to your schedule
  4. Optionally combine with Trading Assistant for position monitoring

Momentum Trading

Momentum trading capitalizes on continuing price trends and market strength:

  • Focuses on assets showing strong directional movement
  • Relies on the principle that trends tend to continue
  • Requires active monitoring and quick decision-making

Implementation with BitGet Bot:

  1. Monitor breakout alerts for strong momentum signals
  2. Filter for "Strong" classification alerts
  3. Confirm momentum with volume and multi-timeframe analysis
  4. Enter positions with the /buy command
  5. Set wider trailing stops to allow for volatility
  6. Example: /buy BTC/USDT 100 followed by /settrailingstop 15 5
